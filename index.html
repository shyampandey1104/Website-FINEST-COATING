🧼 Care Instructions for Long-Lasting Results

🧽

Use Mild Soap

Clean with mild liquid soap and water. Regular, gentle cleaning preserves the finish and extends its life significantly.

🚫

Avoid Abrasives

Never use abrasive cleaners, scouring pads, or steel wool. These can scratch and damage the coating surface.

⚗️

No Harsh Chemicals

Avoid bleach, ammonia, and acid-based cleaners. These chemicals can break down the coating over time.

🧴

Soft Cloth Only

Use a soft cloth or non-abrasive sponge for cleaning. Microfiber cloths work best for maintaining the shine.

💧

Rinse After Use

Rinse the surface after each use and wipe dry when possible. This prevents soap scum and mineral buildup.

🛁

Use Bath Mats

Place non-suction bath mats to prevent scratches. Remove mats after bathing to allow the surface to dry.

🚿

Protect from Impact (Tie-Back)

Painted finishes are prone to chipping. Dropping a heavy metal or plastic hand shower head can easily crack or chip the new coating. Use a zip-tie or a piece of twine to tether the shower hose to the faucet or a grab bar, ensuring that even if the head slips from its bracket, it cannot reach the tub surface.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our bathroom resurfacing services.

How long does bathtub resurfacing take? +
Our same-day bathtub refinishing process takes 4-6 hours to complete. Your refinished bathtub will be ready for use 24 hours once the finish has been completely set.
What is the expected lifespan of bathtub refinishing? +
The newly applied finish on the bathtub will last 5 to 10 years. The amount of daily use, wear, and tear will affect the life of the newly applied finish. We provide care instructions and offer a full 6-month warranty on labor and materials.
What does FC charge for bathtub refinishing? +
Every project is different. The price depends on the amount of work we need to do, the condition of your bathtub, the size of your bathtub, and the color you desire. Other things include any previous work done on the bathtub, or were there any past repairs made. Call us for a free estimate or email us.
Is bathtub refinishing messy? +
Well, that depends on who you hire! FC will protect the other areas of your bathroom by carefully masking off the work area to protect your home or business. After the job is done, we make sure the work area looks as good after we are gone as it did when we arrived.
What types of bathtubs can be refinished? +
We can refinish built-in bathtubs, corner bathtubs, clawfoot bathtubs, drop-in bathtubs, garden bathtubs, Jacuzzi bathtubs, walk-in bathtubs, whirlpool bathtubs, and more. We work with porcelain, fiberglass, acrylic, cast iron, cultured marble, and other materials.
Why is my bathtub peeling after Reglazing? +
When a newly reglazed bathtub starts peeling, it's almost always due to improper preparation and application techniques. Professional reglazing, when done correctly, should last for 7-10 years with proper maintenance.
How does your bathtub refinishing warranty work? +
A warranty is only as good as the company standing behind it. FC has been in the bathtub refinishing business since 2005. We cover our labor and material for a full 6 months of warranty. Note: Our warranty does not cover accidental damage or failure to follow care and maintenance instructions.
How much can I save with refinishing vs replacement? +
You can save up to 75% to 90% versus the cost of replacing your old bathtub by refinishing it. Bathtub refinishing is a great way to restore your existing bathtub so that it looks like new again, without the high cost of bathroom remodeling.
Is FC eco-friendly? +
Yes! At FC we do our best to be as eco-friendly as possible. Reconstruction generates a lot of construction waste with over 50% going into our landfills. Refinishing reduces the amount of construction waste generated since it creates so little waste compared to reconstruction. We use refinishing products that are certified as non-toxic and eco-friendly.
Do you offer commercial services? +
Yes! FC provides end-to-end surface finishing solutions for businesses, architects, and builders. We serve hotels, hospitals, apartment complexes, malls, showrooms, and office complexes. Our services cover powder coating, wood finish on aluminum, and mirror finish coatings. We offer flexible scheduling to minimize disruption to your business.

What is Buffing and Polishing?

🔧
Buffing

Buffing is the deep-cleaning step used to remove dark oxidation, surface imperfections, and buildup. It restores the base shine by eliminating tarnish and scratches from metal surfaces like stainless steel, brass, copper, and bronze.

✨
Polishing

Polishing adds a protective layer and a glossy, mirror-like finish to the surface. It enhances the luster and creates a fingerprint-free, high-gloss appearance that makes your items look brand new.

🪙
Tinning (Kalai)

Tinning is the process of re-lining the inside of copper and brass vessels with tin. This makes the cookware safe for cooking acidic foods and prevents metal contamination, extending the life of your traditional cookware.

Quick Tips for DIY Polishing

🥄
Stainless Steel

For light stains, use a paste of baking soda and water or Bar Keepers Friend. Always buff in the direction of the metal's grain for best results.

🍋
Copper & Brass

A mixture of lemon and salt or tamarind paste is a classic Indian remedy that works instantly to remove oxidation from copper and brass items.

🔪
Knives & Blades

For a polished edge, use a leather strop or very fine-grit whetstone. For the flat blade, metal polish like Flitz works wonders.

How to Maintain After Polishing

🧼
Regular Cleaning

Clean surfaces regularly with a soft cloth and mild detergent. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can scratch the polished finish.

💨
Dry Immediately

After washing, dry metal surfaces immediately to prevent water spots and maintain the shine. Use a microfiber cloth for best results.

🧴
Protective Treatment

Apply a thin layer of mineral oil or specialized metal protectant periodically to maintain the finish and prevent oxidation.

🌡️
Avoid Extreme Heat

Don't expose polished items to extreme temperature changes. For cookware, use medium heat to preserve the finish.
